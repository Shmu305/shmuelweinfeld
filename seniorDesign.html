Millions of people suffer from chronic skin ulcers every year. Chronic wounds are a problem for both the physical wellbeing of a patient as well as for health care providers. An infected wound can cause serious health problems if gone undetected. An infected wound also increases the time it takes for healing and increases the cost and length of a hospital stay. Existing wound monitoring systems require expensive equipment and can also be uncomfortable for patients to wear. Furthermore, many existing wound monitor devices require a trained professional to apply the bandage and review the results. This wound monitoring device is easy to apply and uses sensors that are connected to a flexible and comfortable wound dressing. The sensors constantly monitor the wound health based on pH level, temperature, and pressure. The system can wirelessly send the data through Bluetooth for further analysis. This would not only improve the ability for self-monitoring, but also improve the cost and affordability of home care.
